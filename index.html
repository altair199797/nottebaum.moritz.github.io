About me

I am a computer vision researcher and PhD student in the Machine Learning and Perception Lab at the University of Udine, advised by Christian Micheloni. My research focuses on hardware-efficient neural network design for efficient inference — in particular, building vision backbones that achieve better speed-accuracy trade-offs on real hardware by going beyond traditional multiply-accumulate (MAC) count metrics.

I also work on egocentric vision, video frame interpolation, and object tracking. Previously, I received my M.Sc. in Informatik from TU Darmstadt (2020–2022).
